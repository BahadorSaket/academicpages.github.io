Lawyaw’s Onboarding Project

About

Lawyaw platform is a legal document automation software that helps attorneys save time by automating their document creation and management processes. When I joined the Lawyaw company, one of our main challenges was the high churn rate for our platform. Over 50% of our customers canceled their subscriptions in one or two months after signing up. So, our main goal was to investigate root causes of these early cancelations.

My Role

I was mainly responsible for:

  • 01. investigating ex-customers' interaction logs to indetify the common patterns that resulted in cancelation
  • 02. planning and condcuting remote interviews with our ex-customers
  • 03. generating a stream of insights and recommendations to improve our onboarding process based on the condcuted studies and communicating them to different stakeholders
  • 04. designing a set of analtyical metrics to measure the monthly onboarding success rate
  • 05. collaborating with the data science and engineering teams to implement the onaboarding success metrics on our dahsboards

User Research
Understanding the customers pain points duirng in-person onboarding

Before starting the project we had several meetings with our cross-functional team to better understand the problem our team has been facing and to get a better sense of our customers and their background. We went through some of the comments/feedback we received from some of those who canceled their subscriptions (e.g., ``I find the program too time-consuming''). We also discussed the roadblocks and challenges we were facing with our target users (e.g., attorneys overall lack of familiarity with computer-based technologies). These initial meetings helped me to have a better understanding of the customers, our goals, roadblocks, and expected deadlines.

Understanding user behavior over time

At Lawyaw, we use softwares like MixPanel and Fullstory to monitor users’ interaction with our product over time. I investigated user interaction logs for 10 of our customers who canceled their subscriptions. My goal was to see if I could understand where in the process our customers faced challenges and what roadblocks they encountered that resulted in cancelation. Looking at user logs, what I noticed was that our customers tried creating legal templates using our tool, but they stopped in the middle of the process and did not complete the template creation. After trying multiple templates the customers came to a point (maybe a frustration point?) where they decide to cancel their subscriptions. This was showing us that something was not working as expected. Something was stopping our customers from completing their goals. Our hypothesis was that our customers were facing challenges somewhere in the middle of their process while using our product. This raised multiple interesting questions:

  • 01. Did our customers have difficulties understanding some of our features or designs?
  • 02. Did our platform behave in a way that our customers expected?
  • 03. Did we provide enough tutorials and trainings for our customers during our onboarding?

Talking to Our Ex-customers

To address the hypotheses and questions, we decided to conduct a few remote interviews with five of our ex-customers. We intentionally recruited participants who canceled their subscriptions in the last 1-2 weeks since their memory and experience with our product was still fresh. Our goal was to identify the root causes of these early cancelations. In particular we had a couple of main questions:

  • What are the main challenges and difficulties in using our product?

  • What are the issues and challenges in our onboarding process?

I started the interview by asking some easy warm-up questions (e.g., How did you learn about lawyaw?) to ease the participant into the interview. I then asked my main questions. Below, I listed some of the main questions that I asked during the main phase of the interview.

  • 01. Do you mind walking me through your experience from the time that you subscribed to the time that you canceled?
  • 02. What phase of your experience with our product do you think was frustrating? Why?
  • 03. Do you mind walking me through some of your frustrations?
  • 04. How did the training process look like?
  • 05. Is there anything that we could do to improve this process?

After asking my main questions, I started wrapping-up the interview by asking some easy and general questions or summarizing the interview (e.g., any other thoughts? Any things else that I forgot to ask you?)

Data Analysis

All interviews were conducted remotely using the Zoom software. I invited two other observers (members of our cross-functional team) to our interviews. Both observers were mainly responsible for taking notes during each interview process. After each interview, we had a debrief meeting among ourselves to gain a better understanding of the collected data, identify the meaningful customers’ comments, and clarify any ambiguities or misunderstandings.

After each interview, I took the data from each participant, pulled out the key points (observations, comments, and design ideas) to create an affinity diagram. The affinity diagram helped me to share my main findings to different stakeholders as the study progressed. It also helped me to break down a complicated onboarding issue that we had at Lawyaw into more specific categories.

Findings
One of our main goals during our interviews was to identify when after subscribing to our product, the ex-customers felt frustrated. Our findings shows that our customers usually got frustrated at two phases during the entire process.

First, some of our ex-customers had a hard time to find our training material for a while after subscribing. Right after onboarding, our marketing team shares a few links to the training material through an email, but these links usually got buried through the emails. For example, one of our ex-customers mentioned: ``I did not even know that you have training videos! I expected a link to videos on your website or Lawyaw platform.'' Our customers also faced multiple challenges related to the accessibility, discoverabiliuty, and quality of our training materials.

Hard to access and discover the material

Multiple of our customers mentioned their difficulties in findings or accessing our training materials.

“Also having instructional videos, that would help me. If I could just go somewhere and just type in how to do this and then a video pops up and it walks me through the steps that would help. I don’t think you have such videos.” -- P2

“I think there was a video that I saw, but I feel there were not that many videos to start with. After that I was just trying to read to understand what I need to do.” -- P4

Lack of consistent, high quality, and well-designed training material

“Videos were a little bit jarring. This is no offense to anybody who generated the content. It felt like there were first generation videos and second generation videos. Some of the videos were all over the place and then there were some later ones that had a higher quality. It was showing and highlighting and it was talking as they were doing it. So it felt like someone was in the process of updating the videos.” -- P1

Second, almost all participants found it challenging to work with some of the features in our platform (e.g., setting conditional logics in templates). Part of this frustration was related to the feature designs that were hard to discover and use. In addition, we did not have enough training materials to teach some of the advanced features. So, our ex-customers had to set up a call with our customer service to go through some of the more advanced features. As a result of these frustrations, customers decided to cancel their subscriptions.

Going Forward
Going forward, our team took multiple immediate steps to address the Onboarding related challenges.

  • 01. We created a learning center called "Lawyaw Academy". This unified resource contains all the training resources, webinars, and quizes to help our customers get up and running with the Lawyaw platform right after signing up. We also provided a link to the Academy within the Lawyaw platform.
  • 02. We also created an in-app checkist to get our customers up and running with our product.

  • 03. We designed a satisfaction survey to send to our customers after their onboarding. The main goal of this survey is to investigate the main challenges in our onboarding and idenitfy ways that we could improve the onboarding process.
  • 04. We are currenlty improving the quality of the existing videos and creating some new videos for more advanced features in our product.
  • 05. Over communicating with our customers to ensure they feel supported throughout the onboarding process.
